Week 1 Focus: 50% intensity. All ball work stays middle height, middle of the plate. Build the pattern and groove the swing before adding any challenge. Viz sessions can fall on cage days, off days, or whenever pitchers are throwing.
🛑 Stop Session & Contact PT/MD if any of these occur
  • Sharp or deep anterior shoulder pain during or after swinging
  • Pain > 3/10 at any point
  • New or increased clicking / catching sensation
  • Numbness or tingling down the arm
  • Next-day soreness not resolved within 24 hours

Cage day, off day, or whenever pitchers are throwing · No contact
VisualizationStand-In
Not started
🟡 Stand-In Visualization Protocol Stand in the box vs. machine or live pitcher at game speed. Take every pitch — no swinging. Track the ball out of the hand, time your load, fire your mental trigger, let it pass.
  • 2 rounds of 10 pitches = 20 mental reps minimum
  • Full pre-pitch routine every time (tap, load, track)
  • Optional: take a full dry swing with no ball — rehearse your path, no strain
  • Goal: seeing ball early, trusting timing, staying loose
Rounds
Round 1 — Track & Time
10 pitches. Eyes on release point. Full load — freeze at contact point. Let ball pass.
🟡 10 pitches
2 min — shake out, reset approach
Round 2 — Pitch Recognition
10 pitches. Call ball/strike out loud before the pitch crosses the plate.
🟡 10 pitches
2 min rest

5–10 full dry swings on your best pitch. Full path, no strain. Listen to shoulder.
⚡ Optional · 5–10

🟡 Stand-In with Intermittent Hitting Stand in vs. machine or live pitcher. Take 2 pitches, swing at the 3rd — then repeat. This bridges the gap between visualization and live hitting. You're reading the pitch for real, then trusting your swing when you pull the trigger.
  • Pattern every round: take, take, swing — take, take, swing
  • Full pre-pitch routine on every pitch, even the ones you take
  • Swing at full 80–90% effort — no half-swings when you go
  • Shoulder check between rounds — if anything feels off, drop back to stand-in only
