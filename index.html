Stop Criteria
Level 1 — 45 feet. First full-distance session. Bridge work (20' and 30') is complete — this is the first real volume day. Keep effort easy and controlled. The goal is mechanics quality and tissue tolerance, not arm strength. Note: Bridge session (Fri 3/14) — 30' × 2×20 throws — is complete and noted here for reference.
⚠️ Pre-Session Check — Before You Start
  • If lingering soreness from the prior session does not improve during warm-up movements and warm-up tosses → hold today, try tomorrow
  • Warm-up: 5 min arm circles, band external rotation, shoulder CARs — soreness should settle or reduce, not build
🛑 In-Session Modification Criteria
  • Soreness builds to >4/10: Stop — 5 min full rest — reassess. If it doesn't settle after rest, session is over for the day
  • Sharp pain at any point: Stop immediately. Do not restart. Contact PT before next session
  • Mechanics check — stop or reduce intensity if: Arm path rises above shoulder height on release, short-arming appears under fatigue (loss of full arm arc), trunk starts dragging arm rather than hip/trunk leading, throwing shoulder hikes or fails to decelerate smoothly
🎯 Optimal Mechanics Targets
  • Hip and trunk rotation initiates — arm follows. If the arm is leading, reset
  • Arm path at or below shoulder height through release — no "high and late" pattern
  • Full arm arc maintained across all throws — no short-arming as effort or distance increases
  • Smooth follow-through with controlled deceleration — no abrupt stop or throwing-shoulder hike
